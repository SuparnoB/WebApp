Kolkata: The city of Joy!

Kolkata, Bengali Kalikata, formerly Calcutta , city, capital of West Bengal state, and former capital (1772–1911) of British India. It is one of India’s largest cities and one of its major ports.

Introduction

Kolkata metropolitan city is India’s second largest metropolitan by population and is among one of the four metropolitan cities of India. It is the state capital of West Bengal. Kolkata was earlier known as Calcutta. It is a city made by the British Empire; it was the capital of British India from 1773 to 1911. Kolkata is also nick named as the “City of Joy.” One can experience and explore all the colors of life in this city; it has variety of things to offers from different modes of transfer to different tourist spots to wide variety of foods. Kolkata has something to offer for everyone.

“Calcutta’s the only city I know where you are actively encouraged to stop strangers at random for a quick chat.” – Tahir Shah

Kolkata is the city of festival. Every day is a celebration in Kolkata. The best time to visit the city is during Durga Puja which generally takes place between September and October. During this time of the year the entire city is light up with millions of lights. It is decorated like a princess to welcome Maa Durga. Kolkata is famous for its desserts and sweets Mishţi Doi, Shondesh, Roshogolla, Laddu, Pantua, Chomchom, Piţha or pithe are some of the famous sweets of Kolkata. Kolkata’s street food is one of a kind, these street food are generally very cheap, you can have mouthwatering muri, puchka, aalo-dum just for rupees ten!
